The planets!

What is a Planet?

A planet is:

  • a - it must orbit a star (in our cosmic neighbourhood, the Sun)
  • b - it must be big enough to have enough gravity to force into a spehrical shape.
  • c - it must be big enough that its gravity has cleared away any other objects of a similar size near its orbit around the Sun.

Our solar System has 8 beautiful planets divided into two groups- inner plantes or terrestrial planets and outer planets or jovian planets based on their composition accordingly

Inner planets or Terrestrial planets:

    1. Mercury:

  • Mercury is the smallest planet in our solar system and is the closest to the Sun.
  • A day on Mercury is longer than a year, meaning it takes Mercury longer to rotate on its axis than it does to orbit the Sun.
  • Mercury's craters are named after artists, musicians, and authors
  • Mercury is still shrinking due to its molten, iron core cooling down

    • 2. Venus:

  • Venus is the hottest planet in our solar system, with an average surface temperature of 462°C (862°F)
  • Venus has a thick atmosphere that traps heat, creating a runaway greenhouse effect.
  • A day on Venus is longer than its year (243 Earth days to rotate, 225 Earth days to orbit).
  • Venus is named after the Roman goddess of love and beauty

    • 3. Earth:

  • Earth is the only planet known to harbor life.
  • Earth's atmosphere protects us from meteoroids and radiation from the Sun.
  • Earth has a unique chemical composition, unlike any other planet in our solar system.
  • Earth's magnetic field protects us from harmful solar radiation.

    • 4. Mars:

  • Mars has the largest volcano in the solar system, Olympus Mons
  • There have been more missions to Mars than any other planet.
  • Mars has a thin atmosphere, making it very cold and dry.
  • Mars has polar ice caps made of water ice and carbon dioxide ice.

Outer planets or Jovian planets:

    1. Jupiter

  • Jupiter is the largest planet in our solar system, with a mass more than double that of all the other planets combined.
  • Jupiter has a Great Red Spot, a giant storm that has been raging for over 300 years.
  • Jupiter has a strong magnetic field, which is the strongest in the solar system.
  • Jupiter has more than 95 moons.

    • 2. Saturn

  • Saturn is famous for its prominent ring system, which are made up of ice and rock particles.
  • Saturn has more moons than any other planet in the solar system.
  • Saturn is the second largest planet in our solar system.
  • Saturn's moon Titan has a dense atmosphere and liquid methane lakes and rivers.

    • 3. Uranus:

  • Uranus rotates on its side, with a nearly 90-degree tilt from the plane of its orbit.
  • Uranus is an ice giant, composed of water, methane, and ammonia.
  • Uranus has faint rings and 28 small moons.
  • Uranus was the first planet discovered with the aid of a telescope.

    • 4. Neptune:

  • Neptune is the farthest planet from the Sun.
  • Neptune has the fastest wind speeds in the solar system.
  • Neptune is an ice giant, similar to Uranus.
  • Neptune has a Great Dark Spot, a storm similar to Jupiter's Great Red Spot.

These eight planets are the wealth and pride of our galaxy showing how perfectly each planet and its characteristics resemble the extraordinary life and colorfulness of our galaxy
