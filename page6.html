Other Substances found in Outer Space

Gasses:

    Hydrogen and Helium:

  • These are the most abundant elements in the universe, forming a plasma that permeates interstellar space.

    • Other gasses:

  • Beyond hydrogen and helium, outer space also contains molecules like water (H2O), ammonia (NH3), and methanol (CH3OH), as well as other complex organic molecules.

    • Cosmic Rays:

  • These are high-energy particles, including ionized atomic nuclie and various subatomic particles, that ravel through space.

Dust particles:

    Cosmic Dust:

  • This is made up of tiny particles of rock, metal, and ice, often referred to as "space dust".

    • Interstellar Medium:

  • This the material that exists in the space between stars in a galaxy, including gas, dust, and particles.

    • Nebulae:

  • These are large clouds of gas and dust, often containing hydrogen atoms.

    • Meteors and Asteroids:

  • Small bodies of rock, metal, and ice that orbit the sun.
